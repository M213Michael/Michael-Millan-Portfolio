About

I am a mechanical engineer based in Northern California who designs, builds, and validates his own work. It started with tinkering, a Traxxas RC car that spiraled into a lifelong habit of taking things apart and making them better, and turned serious when I learned MIG, TIG, and stick welding at Merced College. I hold a BS in Mechanical Engineering from UC Merced and a Welding Technology certificate from Merced College.

What I think sets me apart is that I can take a design the whole way myself: model it, predict where it will fail, fabricate it, test it in the real world, and iterate. I taught myself ANSYS FEA, 3D scanning, and scan-to-CAD reverse engineering, and I use them to identify failure modes and predict them with real accuracy. My skid plate work is the clearest example, where the simulation called the failure location before the part ever broke.

At O&M Industries I produce full SolidWorks drawing packages for industrial fabrication clients including Tesla, Ceco Environmental, Adwest, and Nestec. The company gives engineers SolidWorks at home and shop access on request, and I have made the most of that, building my off-road truck projects into a continuous design, analysis, and fabrication portfolio. My main constraint right now is shop access, and I am looking for a role that puts a fuller set of tools in my hands. I am open to relocating to California, Nevada, or Arizona for a design or analysis-driven role.
